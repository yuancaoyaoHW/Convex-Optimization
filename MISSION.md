# Mission: Convex Optimization for Research

## Why
Learn convex optimization deeply enough to use it as a research tool: recognizing when a problem has exploitable convex structure, translating research questions into optimization models, and understanding the theory behind algorithms and duality.

## Success looks like
- Identify convex structure in a new research problem and explain why it matters.
- Reformulate common non-obvious problems into convex optimization problems when possible.
- Read papers that use convex optimization, duality, and interior-point methods without treating them as black boxes.
- Choose appropriate theory, modeling tricks, or algorithms for a research task.

## Constraints
- Pace: one book chapter per day, with short lessons and review artifacts.
- Background: undergraduate mathematics major; linear algebra, calculus, probability, and real analysis are available foundations.
- Primary text: `bv_cvxbook.pdf`, Boyd and Vandenberghe, _Convex Optimization_.

## Out of scope
- Coding-heavy solver tutorials before the core theory is in place.
- Exhaustive exercise solving for every chapter.
- Nonconvex optimization theory except where the book uses it for contrast.
